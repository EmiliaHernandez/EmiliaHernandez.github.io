About

UCF's official welcome week has a micro-site dedicated to covering the events and sponsors. This site has gone through development in its last two runs by parter on the web design team at UCF. My specific contributions to the site were the illustrations on the Home, About, and Events pages. The preview above was my concept, which was later programmed collaboratively. Not only that, but my teammate and I created Lo-fi and Hi-fi mockups of the sites using Figma. We also accessed the site's analytics to determine our strengths and weaknesses and consider them on our second run. Through this, we were able to make smarter decisions such as implementing keywords, using other campus sites we manage to promote it, and wrapping up earlier than last year so as to get early buzz and have less people accessing outdated content. The 2022 project took 1 month to complete, and was successfully launched in June, about two months before the event. A side-by-side comparison of this project's redesign can be found here


*Any graphics and photography displayed inside the page content was created by other OSI Creative services staff

=======

The UCF Office of Student Involvement's website, again is managed my myself and my teammate. The preview displays a webpage that I developed solo, from a design request made by the student director of the agency being represented. Through my role as web designer, I receive orders for desired changes on the page and sometimes there is complete creative freedom, and other times there are requirements, such as with this design! Originally, content was spread across three links: home, DJ roster, and events. The redesign's main goal was to convert this agency's information into a nice-looking one-pager since every link held little content. This was consolidated into one page and this is the finished project. Specifically, the "upcoming events" and "dj roster" was developed by myself.

=======

Through employment in the UCF design group, Student Government is one of four webpages that are maintained, updated, and improved upon by myself and my teammate. The initiative tracker was built by myself last year, containing all the plans that the administration has for student life. Periodically, the chief of staff lets me know what initiatives have a change in status, and it's my responsibility to reflect that on the site. Student government places the most orders, often with leadership changes, documentation replacements, and student-life updates. The initiative tracker previously displayed the full description of the initiative, and it was requested to change it to a responsive drop-down. This is the live product, which constantly changes as initiatives are completed.
